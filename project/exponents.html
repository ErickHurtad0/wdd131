Legendary Blues Exponents

We celebrate the legendary artists who shaped the soul of the blues, the modern ones, and those who were influenced by this precious genre.

Classics

B.B. King

Known as the “King of the Blues,” B.B. King’s expressive vibrato and emotional phrasing changed guitar playing forever.

  • The Thrill Is Gone
  • Lucille
  • Every Day I Have the Blues

Muddy Waters

The man who electrified the blues. His Chicago sound became the foundation for rock ’n’ roll itself.

  • I'm Your Hoochie Coochie Man
  • Mannish Boy
  • Got My Mojo Working

Robert Johnson

According to legend, Robert Johnson met a large figure — the Devil — at a cemetery. The Devil took his guitar, tuned it, played a few songs, and then returned it to Johnson, granting him mastery of the instrument.

  • Walkin' Blues
  • Me and the Devil Blues
  • They're Red Hot

Modern

Tinsley Ellis

Inspired by a live appearance by B.B. King, where King broke a guitar string and gave it to a 15-year-old Ellis - which Ellis claims to still have in his possession to this day - he was determined to become a blues guitarist.

  • In From The Cold
  • Kiss of Death
  • Freeway Soul

Mighty Sam McClain

Samuel McClain, better known as Mighty Sam early in his career, and later billed as Mighty Sam McClain, conquered the modern scene as a soul blues singer and songwriter.

  • When The Hurt Is Over
  • I'm Tired Of These Blues
  • Don't Worry About Me

Derek Trucks

His music is rooted in blues and rock, and has appeared twice in Rolling Stone's list of "The 100 Greatest Guitarists of All Time".

  • On The Road Again
  • Will The Circle Be Unbroken
  • Rust

Highly influenced By Blues

The Rolling Stones

Often considered the quintessential rock band, the Rolling Stones drew heavily from blues music, covering songs by blues legends like Muddy Waters and Howlin' Wolf.

  • Midnight Rambler
  • Stop Breaking Down
  • Little Red Rooster

The Doors

Their music blends rock, blues, and psychedelia, with Jim Morrison's lyrics often reflecting blues themes of existentialism and longing.

  • Roadhouse Blues
  • The WASP
  • Been Down So Long

Fleetwood Mac

Originally formed as a blues band, Fleetwood Mac's early work featured a strong blues influence, particularly in their Peter Green-led era.

  • Jumping at Shadows
  • Hi Ho Silver
  • Dust My Broom
