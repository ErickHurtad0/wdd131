History Of Blues

The blues was born in the Deep South of the United States during the late 19th century. Rooted in African-American spirituals, work songs, and folk traditions, it became the emotional voice of struggle, hope, and resilience.

Music Theory

At its heart, the blues is built on the 12-bar structure, expressive chord progressions, and the “blue notes” that bend traditional scales to convey deep emotion and soul. Learn the patterns that define this timeless sound.

Blues Exponents

From legends like B.B. King and Muddy Waters to modern artists such as Gary Clark Jr., the blues continues to evolve. Explore the musicians who shaped its past and those carrying the torch today.
